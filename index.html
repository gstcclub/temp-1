PROJECT PRESENTATION

An investigation into the structural integrity of digital archival systems and the socio-technical implications of metadata entropy in the twenty-first century.

LAST UPDATED: 11.2024
LOCATION: ZÜRICH, CH

PROJECT CONCEPT

The methodology focuses on the intersections of information theory and architectural conservation. By applying rigorous data-modeling techniques to historical archives, we aim to uncover latent patterns in digital decay.

Our research utilizes a multi-layered approach, combining quantitative analysis of dataset integrity with qualitative observations of system interfaces. This dual perspective allows for a holistic understanding of how information is preserved, transformed, or lost across temporal boundaries.
